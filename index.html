The Road to Developer.

The early days.

I was exposed to my first command lines in the late 1980s. I had the chance to learn on an early Army command and control system that was UNIX based. Horribly difficult to work with, the user-manual was 6 inches thick and 8pt print. Thankfully that soon gave way to Linux and later better GUI controls.


Point and Click!

The PC revolution of the early 90s, with its mouse and point/click, soon put an end to my command line journey. I started more robust combat operations in those days and soon moved away from computers all together except as an end user.

The mouse really SUNK my command line usage.


Modern Times.

In 2004 I accepted an unpaid gig as an Administrator of a very large forum. This forum was based on a software called Vbulletin. .php, CSS, and some MySQL allowed me to get a taste of the coding environment. I wasnt coding in the sense of CREATING anything I was just asked to help tweak a nav bar here, or a profile field there.


Well, that did it, since 2004 Ive administrated, moderated, or simply owned my own forums. Vbulletin and now Xenforo are my preferable platforms.

My favorite forum software is vbulletin Xenforo!

All of those projects failed mainly because it was impossible to compete with Reddit, Facebook and Twitter.

Those huge platforms were well beyond my REACH to copy.


Here I am now. I want to learn more about front end and mobile development and add to my meager back-end skills. I have an empty forum now at Talk and Stuff! I will add a responsive home page and develope a mobile app for that forum, some day.
